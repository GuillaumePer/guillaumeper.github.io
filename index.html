Facial Recognition & Compressed Sensing

For most Humans, there is no major issue when it comes to recognize people or objects that surround us in our daily life. Indeed we have been identifying, classifying/labelling and memorizing objects and faces since our childhood. But what happens when it comes to computers or machines ?

Nevertheless, face and object recognition is a central issue for industry and our societies nowadays for security reasons and for industrial automation. Computer Face and Object Recognition has thus become of the highest interest for many tangible applications in recent years.

Some recent attempts related to Deep Learning methods have been successful to reach that goal but at relatively hight costs. Therefore, an approach based on Compressed Sensing, proposed n "", has thus been implemented and confirmed the feasibility of such a method which takes advantage of the sparsity of the retrieved solutions. Sparsity of Representation for Classification - SRC- is designed in such a way that it is robust to real life conditions such as variations in lighting, change in facial expressions, and face occlusion and data corruption.

Tests were run using 2 face databases:

  1. Yale B Database ("CroppedYale") - a collection of 39 categories with up to 64 variations in lighting;
  2. Yalefaces Database - a collection of 15 categories with 11 variations (lighting, facial expressions, adds-on (glasses)).

The success of the method has also been proven for object recognition for object databases such as the one created by the ALOI and available at following this link

Network analysis for Marketing Purposes

Nowadays social media such as Twitter have become a reality not only in the news media but also in the marketing business. Lots of companies use those means to promote events, their products and their services. They nurture their branding and reputation using their social media accounts as a prevailing platform of communication.

But sometimes, those can be turnt around and serve as a public floor for customers facing operating issues with some companies to express their disagreement and sometimes huge disappointment. This project's goal was thus to study the spread of this kind of information throughout twitter's network, to understand how it becomes viral news and how this could affect a company's image and even its internal policies.
